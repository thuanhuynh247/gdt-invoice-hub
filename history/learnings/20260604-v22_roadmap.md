## 1. Architectural Lessons & Takeaways

### A. Statutory Penalty Simulation (Decree 125/2020/NĐ-CP)
- **Lesson:** Businesses face high financial risks from compliance audit delays. By implementing an automated, configurable penalty evaluator that models Vietnam's statutory rates (20% under-declaration penalty, 0.03% daily late interest, and 1x-3x evasion penalty factors), corporations can pre-audit their ledgers and build defense reserves.
- **Pattern:** Using explicit `due_date` boundaries and date arithmetic to compute elapsed days accurately ensures that interest calculations mirror the tax authority's audit logic.

### B. E-Commerce Platform Data Normalization
- **Lesson:** API and export schemas from Shopee, Lazada, and TikTok Shop vary widely in field names, pricing net/gross layouts, and commission formats. Standardizing these into a single unified `EcommerceOrder` schema *prior* to reconciliation simplifies downstream compliance checks.
- **Pattern:** Mapping transactions against buyer/seller e-invoices with a small tolerance (1,000 VND) handles minor rounding errors on shipping fees or platform discounts without raising false alarms.

## 2. Technical Decisions & Refinements

### A. Progressive PIT Computations & Form 05/QTT-TNCN Export
- **Sliding-scale brackets:** Vietnam's PIT uses a 7-tier progressive system. Implementing this using a structured array of bounds and tax rates makes the calculation easy to test, inspect, and maintain compared to nested if-statements.
- **Valid XML Compiling:** General Department of Taxation (GDT) XML structures for Form 05/QTT-TNCN must follow strict layout hierarchies (such as `<HSoThueDTu>`). Validating generated XML files against mock schemas protects users from submission errors on the official HTKK portal.

## 3. Critical Patterns Promoted

1. **Statutory Tax Interest Calculation (0.03% daily):**
   - Always verify dates and exclude the invoice date itself, commencing the 0.03% accrual precisely on `due_date + 1`.
2. **Unified E-Commerce Normalization Pattern:**
   - Map platform-specific keys (e.g. Lazada's `gross_revenue` vs Shopee's item price) into a standardized transaction ledger to isolate normalization from matching algorithms.
